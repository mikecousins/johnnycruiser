  I have always been interested in tiny countries, from the days when I collected stamps from Andorra, San Marino, Liechtenstein, etc. I wanted to add the small countries of the world to my travel bucket list, but where do you draw the line? What makes a country a "small country"? Realizing that my home town, Calgary, is quite a good size, I decided that I would determine a country to be "small" if it would fit into Calgary (maybe with a bit of squeezing). Surfing the Web turned up 24 countries that fit the bill (no pun intended) which was a reasonable load to add to my bucket list. I did find discrepancies relating to the status of some countries (ie. is it or is it not a country?) and differing estimates of the total area of the countries. However, I have selected the countries as I have best seen fit. Below is a list of these tiny nations and my visiting progress to date.
